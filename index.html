Biography

About Me

Karen Arias is a Venezuelan flutist whose warm and expressive sound has become her artistic signature. Born in Carúpano, Venezuela — a coastal town with a deep Caribbean identity — she grew up surrounded by rhythms, colors, and a culture where music is a fundamental language.

She began her flute studies at the age of seven, and by ten she was selected to continue her musical training in Caracas at the Simón Bolívar Conservatory and the National Flute Academy. Her artistic formation blends classical discipline with an emotional depth shaped by teachers such as Vicente Urbaez, Raimundo Pineda, Elvis Villarroel, and Miguel Pineda.

Karen has been part of orchestras in both Venezuela and Argentina, including the Orquesta Juvenil Municipal de San Martín and the Sinfónica Juvenil Nacional Libertador San Martín. She has also participated in symphonic and theatrical productions such as Principito Sinfónico and Navidad en las Películas.

Now based in Buenos Aires, she continues refining her craft while shaping a musical identity that fuses Venezuelan roots, classical rigor, and contemporary rhythms. Her vision is simple yet powerful: for her music to be an emotional refuge for those who listen.
